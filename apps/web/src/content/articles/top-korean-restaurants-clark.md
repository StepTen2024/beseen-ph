# Top 10 Korean Restaurants in Clark for Authentic K-Food

Here's something not everyone knows: Clark has one of the largest Korean expat communities in the Philippines. There are Korean international schools, Korean churches, Korean grocery stores, and‚Äîmost importantly for us‚Äîa ton of authentic Korean restaurants.

Forget the Westernized Korean places in Manila. Out here, Korean restaurants are run by Koreans, for Koreans. And that means the food is the real deal.

---

## Why Clark for Korean Food?

Korean investment in Clark Freeport Zone has been significant for decades. There are Korean business owners, retirees, families with kids in Korean schools, and a steady flow of tourists. This community supports restaurants that don't have to water down their flavors for the Western palate.

When a Korean restaurant in Clark serves kimchi jjigae, it tastes like it would in Seoul. That's the difference.

### 1. Sowon Korean Restaurant
**Location:** Clark Freeport Zone  
**Price Range:** ‚Ç±400-800/person  
**Best For:** All-around authentic Korean

Sowon is the name that comes up most often when you ask long-term expats where to get Korean food. It's been around for years, the quality is consistent, and the banchan (side dishes) are generous.

**Must order:** Samgyeopsal (pork belly BBQ), Kimchi Jjigae, and their Japchae is excellent.

**The vibe:** Casual Korean restaurant with BBQ tables. Nothing fancy, just good food.

---

### 2. Sariwon Korean BBQ
**Location:** Marquee Mall, Angeles City  
**Price Range:** ‚Ç±500-900/person  
**Best For:** Korean BBQ in a mall setting

Need Korean BBQ but also need air conditioning and parking? Sariwon in Marquee Mall delivers. It's a chain, but a good one‚Äîunlimited BBQ with quality meat cuts and attentive service.

**Must order:** The unlimited samgyupsal set. Also try their Army Stew (Budae Jjigae) if you're with a group.

**The vibe:** Modern, clean, family-friendly. Good for groups.

---

### 3. Mr. Kimchi
**Location:** Clark  
**Price Range:** ‚Ç±300-600/person  
**Best For:** Casual Korean, good value

Mr. Kimchi is where you go when you want solid Korean food without the premium price. The portions are big, the prices are reasonable, and it's popular with both Koreans and Filipinos.

**Must order:** Bibimbap (mixed rice bowl), Tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes), and their fried chicken.

**The vibe:** Fast-casual, good for lunch or quick dinner.

---

### 4. K-BBQ House
**Location:** Near Clark Airport  
**Price Range:** ‚Ç±400-700/person  
**Best For:** Quick BBQ near the airport

Got a layover or waiting for a flight? K-BBQ House is close to Clark Airport and serves up quick, tasty Korean BBQ. Not the fanciest spot, but reliable and convenient.

**Must order:** Beef bulgogi, LA galbi (short ribs).

**The vibe:** No-frills, get-in-get-out Korean BBQ.

---

### 5. Hangout Korean Restaurant
**Location:** Friendship Highway, Angeles City  
**Price Range:** ‚Ç±350-600/person  
**Best For:** Casual Korean with drinking food

Hangout does Korean "anju" (drinking food) really well. Think fried chicken, Korean pancakes, and dishes meant to go with soju or beer.

**Must order:** Korean Fried Chicken, Seafood Pajeon (pancake), and a bottle of soju.

**The vibe:** Casual Korean pub atmosphere. Good for evening hangouts.

---

### 6. Seoul House
**Location:** Clark Freeport Zone  
**Price Range:** ‚Ç±400-800/person  
**Best For:** Traditional Korean dishes

Seoul House focuses on traditional Korean cooking beyond just BBQ. If you want dishes like Galbitang (short rib soup), Samgyetang (ginseng chicken soup), or Haemul Pajeon, this is your spot.

**Must order:** Samgyetang‚Äîespecially good if you're feeling under the weather.

**The vibe:** More traditional Korean restaurant feel.

---

### 7. Masil Charcoal Grill
**Location:** Clark  
**Price Range:** ‚Ç±500-900/person  
**Best For:** Premium Korean BBQ experience

Masil uses real charcoal for their grills, which makes a noticeable difference in the flavor. They're a step up in price, but the meat quality matches.

**Must order:** Premium beef cuts, grilled pork jowl.

**The vibe:** Slightly upscale, good for date nights.

---

### 8. Dong Won Garden
**Location:** Clark  
**Price Range:** ‚Ç±400-700/person  
**Best For:** Local Korean community favorite

Dong Won is where actual Korean residents go for a taste of home. Less marketed to tourists, more of a neighborhood Korean restaurant. The menu is in Korean and English, and the auntie running the place knows what she's doing.

**Must order:** Whatever the daily special is‚Äîthey know what's fresh.

**The vibe:** Homestyle Korean, local community spot.

---

### 9. BBQ Chicken Clark
**Location:** SM City Clark  
**Price Range:** ‚Ç±300-500/person  
**Best For:** Korean Fried Chicken

Part of the Korean fried chicken franchise, BBQ Chicken does exactly what you expect: crispy, flavorful Korean fried chicken in various flavors. The honey garlic and spicy varieties are both solid.

**Must order:** Half-and-half chicken (two flavors), pickled radish on the side.

**The vibe:** Fast-casual, good for takeout or quick dine-in.

---

### 10. Jumong Korean Restaurant
**Location:** Angeles City  
**Price Range:** ‚Ç±350-600/person  
**Best For:** Budget-friendly Korean

Named after the Korean historical drama, Jumong offers good Korean food at lower prices than most. It's popular with students and budget-conscious diners who still want authentic flavors.

**Must order:** Bibimbap, Jajangmyeon (black bean noodles), Korean fried dumplings.

**The vibe:** Casual, affordable, no-frills.

## Final Tips

1. **Learn basic Korean food names** - Makes ordering easier and shows respect
2. **Don't fill up on banchan** - Save room for the main dishes
3. **Soju is meant to be shared** - Don't pour your own glass
4. **Lettuce wraps are your friend** - Wrap that meat properly
5. **Ask for recommendations** - Korean restaurant owners love when you ask what's good
