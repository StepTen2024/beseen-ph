# Cost of Living in Angeles City - Expat's Budget Breakdown

One of the first questions anyone asks when considering Angeles City: "How much does it cost to live there?"

The answer, like most things, is: it depends. But we can give you real numbers based on how people actually live here‚Äînot some fantasy budget or luxury lifestyle estimate.

---

## The Quick Answer

**Comfortable living for a single expat:** ‚Ç±60,000-100,000/month ($1,100-1,800 USD)

**Budget living (local style):** ‚Ç±30,000-50,000/month ($550-900 USD)

**Upscale living (condos, nice restaurants):** ‚Ç±120,000-200,000+/month ($2,200-3,600+ USD)

These ranges assume you're renting, not buying. Now let's break it down.

## üè† Housing

Housing is one of the biggest variables. You can pay ‚Ç±8,000/month for a basic apartment or ‚Ç±60,000+ for a luxury condo. Most expats fall somewhere in between.

### Apartments / Houses for Rent

| Type | Monthly Rent | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Studio apartment (basic) | ‚Ç±8,000-15,000 | Local-style, maybe no AC in bedroom |
| 1BR apartment (decent) | ‚Ç±15,000-25,000 | With AC, decent area |
| 2BR apartment (nice) | ‚Ç±25,000-40,000 | Good for couples, proper kitchen |
| House (3BR) | ‚Ç±30,000-60,000 | Depends heavily on location |
| Condo (modern) | ‚Ç±25,000-50,000 | In Clark area, newer buildings |
| Condo (upscale) | ‚Ç±50,000-80,000+ | Amenities, pool, security |

### Best Areas for Expats
- **Malabanias** - Popular, lots of amenities, moderate prices
- **Clark Freeport Zone** - Modern condos, secure, pricier
- **Balibago** - Close to action, mixed quality
- **Anunas** - Quieter, more residential, good value

### Utilities
- **Electricity:** ‚Ç±3,000-8,000/month (depends on AC usage‚Äîand you WILL use AC)
- **Water:** ‚Ç±500-1,000/month
- **Internet:** ‚Ç±1,500-3,000/month (Converge, PLDT, or Globe)
- **Cable TV:** ‚Ç±500-1,500/month (or just use streaming)

**Note:** Electricity is expensive in the Philippines. Running AC all day can push your bill to ‚Ç±10,000+.
